Your Mindset on Shoot Day
  • You are a storyteller first, cameraman second. Capture emotion, not just action.
  • Always be moving. Dead still = boring clips.
  • When in doubt, go lower and closer. Low angles = cinematic. Tight = emotion.
  • Shoot more than you think you need. You can always cut, you can't go back.
  • The best clips are unexpected — a laugh, a reaction, a look. Be ready.
Sony a7 Setup for Game Day
Shooting Mode
Continuous Burst (Hi+) for action shots · Video mode for clips
💡 Burst catches the exact peak moment — the flag pull, the jump catch.
Shutter Speed
1/1000s minimum for fast action
💡 Faster shutter = frozen motion = sharp stills. Go higher in bright sun.
Aperture
f/2.8–f/4 for blurred backgrounds · f/5.6+ for wide multi-player shots
💡 Low f-stop = that cinematic background blur on close-ups.
ISO
Keep under 3200 outdoors · Auto ISO is fine in good light
💡 Higher ISO = grainy clips. Natural light is your friend.
Video Frame Rate
60fps for everything
💡 60fps lets you slow down ANY clip in edit. Don't shoot at 30fps.
Video Resolution
4K if possible · 1080p minimum
💡 4K gives you crop flexibility when editing vertical Reels from horizontal footage.
Picture Profile
S-Log2 or S-Log3 if comfortable · Standard if not
💡 S-Log = more color grading range. If you're not editing, stick to Standard.
Stabilization (IBIS)
ON — always
💡 You'll be moving around constantly. Shaky footage kills the cinematic feel.

Shot List by Priority

Every shot below should be on your mental checklist during the game.

🔴 Must Get
The Flag Pull
📐 Low angle, sideline level
Camera low to the ground, wide enough to catch the runner AND the defender going for the flag. The moment of the pull = freeze-worthy.
🎯 Think ESPN sideline cam — grass in foreground, action mid-frame.
QB Release
📐 Slightly behind, tight telephoto
Catch the QB's arm coming forward and the ball mid-air. Golden if you get the receiver in the same frame.
🎯 NFL Films style — slow mo potential. Get this on burst mode.
TD Celebration
📐 Run toward the player — get close
The second a TD is scored, [You ~ Media] moves. Get the chest pump, the yell, the team mob. This is the clip everyone shares.
🎯 Shoot vertical for Reels. Stay close. Show emotion, not just the action.
Pre-snap Lineup
📐 End zone perspective, looking down the field
From behind the offense looking down the field. Shows the whole formation. Great for the cinematic feel.
🎯 Like a video game camera angle. Wide lens, still frame.
🟠 Great to Get
Player Close-up
📐 Tight on the face — between plays
Catch players hyping each other up, calling plays, catching their breath. Real unscripted moments.
🎯 Candid. Don't stage it. This is the soul of the content.
Running Route
📐 Tracking/follow shot
Follow a receiver from the snap through their route break. Shows athleticism.
🎯 Requires smooth panning. Practice the movement before game time.
Interception / Big D Play
📐 Stay wide, react fast
You won't always know it's coming — stay a little wider to catch the unexpected defensive play.
🎯 These are the "holy shit" clips. Worth the wide trade-off.
🔵 Bonus
Pregame Arrival
📐 Candid, walking shots
Players arriving, lacing up, tossing the ball around. Sets the tone for a highlight reel opener.
🎯 Shoot in burst. Pick the 2–3 cleanest stills.
Team Huddle
📐 Slightly elevated or sneak inside the angle
Get the energy of the huddle — show the team together. Short 5-second clip.
🎯 Perfect Reel intro or highlight reel transition.
Mic & Player Cam Strategy

Tagging players with mics is where you get the content that separates good leagues from great ones. Pick 2–3 players per game. Ideally someone loud, funny, or a big personality.

How to Brief the Miked Player

Tell them this before the game:

"Hey, you're mic'd up today. Just play your game — forget the mic is there. But if you score, call a play, or talk trash, own it. Between plays, talk to teammates like normal. We're not scripting anything."
Key Mic Moments to Chase
Pre-game trash talk / hype
⏱ Before kickoff
💡 Ask 1 player: "What's your prediction today?" — gold every time.
Mid-game reaction after a big play
⏱ Immediately after a TD or big stop
💡 Run up right away. "How'd that feel?" Short answer = best content.
QB explaining the TD play
⏱ Sideline break
💡 Have them draw the route with their hands in the air. Fans love this.
Post-game raw reaction
⏱ The moment the game ends
💡 Win or lose — raw emotion is the best content. Don't overthink it, just roll.
Following the Player — Camera Movement
  • Stay 5–8 feet behind the player on the sideline. Close enough for audio, far enough for a good frame.
  • When they go on the field, track them with your lens from the sideline — you can't follow on field.
  • Film their sideline reactions after every play they're involved in.
  • Get their face AND their teammates reacting to them. Two angles of emotion = better story.
Reels & TikTok — Structure

Target length: 20–35 seconds. Every second needs to earn its place.

0–2s
HOOK
Best play of the day OR a face cam player hype moment. NO slow builds. Hit them instantly.
2–8s
MONTAGE
3–4 rapid cuts: big play → celebration → another play. Fast-paced, music-synced.
8–15s
MONEY SHOT
Your cleanest slow-mo clip here — flag pull, TD catch, QB release. Let it breathe.
15–25s
STORY MOMENT
Player mic moment, a reaction, or a huddle clip. Humanizes the content and hooks followers.
25–35s
OUTRO
League name/logo, a hype text overlay, or a call to action. Keep it clean — one element only.
The Formula That Works

Hook instantly → Build fast → Hit the slow mo → End with a reaction.

The music drop should line up with your best play. Edit to the beat — cut on the downbeat. Every frame should feel intentional.

Highlight Reel — Full Cut

Target length: 2–3 minutes. Lives on YouTube + Instagram + split for TikTok.

INTRO — 0:00–0:20
Pregame footage + hype music. Show the venue, players warming up, the vibe.
🎬 Director tip: Use a cinematic wide shot to open. No text overlays yet.
ACT 1 – FIRST HALF — 0:20–1:00
Best 6–8 plays from the first half. Mix action with reactions and celebrations.
🎬 Director tip: Every 3rd clip should be a reaction to break up the action.
ACT 2 – MIC MOMENTS — 1:00–1:30
2–3 mic clips back to back. Let the player's personality come through.
🎬 Director tip: Add player name lower-thirds (text overlays) here.
ACT 3 – SECOND HALF — 1:30–2:30
Build to the biggest plays. Save your absolute best clip for the 2:20 mark.
🎬 Director tip: Slow mo on the clutch moments. Let the best play breathe.
OUTRO — 2:30–2:45
Final score, player of the game, league branding. Simple and clean.
🎬 Director tip: Always end on a smile or celebration. Leave people feeling good.

Player Spotlight Videos

Short 60–90 second videos built around one player. Think "Player of the Week." Tag them, they share it, your league grows.

Spotlight Video Structure
0–5sPlayer name + number + position as a text overlay on a sick action shot. Cinematic opener.
5–30sTheir best 3–4 plays from the game. Slow mo on the highlight play.
30–50sMic moment or a quick 10-second interview clip. Shows personality.
50–70sStat line of the game — catches, TDs, flags pulled — as animated text overlays.
70–80sLeague branding + tag the player's IG handle. Call to action.
Best Spotlight Subject Checklist
  • Had a big game — 2+ TDs or multiple flag pulls
  • Has a big personality on camera — energy translates
  • Is active on social — they'll share it and tag friends
  • Is a recognizable player your audience already follows
Quick Interview Questions (1 take, no script)
"Walk me through that TD — what did you see?"
"How's the team feeling going into next week?"
"Who on your team should people be watching?"
"What's your goal for the rest of the season?"

Color & Edit Style Summary
COLOR GRADE
Warm + punchy. Boost oranges/yellows on skin tones. Deep shadows. Cinematic contrast.
SLOW MO
Use sparingly — only on the best 2–3 moments per video. Overuse kills impact.
CUTS
Fast on montage sections. Let mic + reaction moments breathe with longer clips.
TEXT OVERLAYS
Bold, simple, all caps. Player name + stat only. No clutter, no busy graphics.
